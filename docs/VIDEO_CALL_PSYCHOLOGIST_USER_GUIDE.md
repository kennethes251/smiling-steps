## üé• Conducting Video Sessions

### Joining the Call

**Step 1: Access Your Dashboard**
- Log into your Smiling Steps account
- Navigate to your session dashboard
- Find the upcoming session

**Step 2: Review Client Information**
- Check client name and session details
- Review any previous session notes
- Ensure payment is confirmed

**Step 3: Join the Session**
- Click "Join Call" (available 15 minutes before)
- Allow camera and microphone permissions
- Wait for client to join (they may need technical help)

### Session Interface

**Your Video Display**
- Your video appears in a small window (usually top-right)
- Client's video fills the main screen
- You can adjust your video size and position

**Control Panel (Bottom of Screen)**
- **Microphone**: Mute/unmute yourself
- **Camera**: Turn your video on/off
- **Screen Share**: Share documents or resources
- **End Call**: Conclude the session
- **Settings**: Adjust audio/video quality

**Session Information**
- **Timer**: Shows current session duration
- **Connection Status**: Green = good, yellow = fair, red = poor
- **Participant Status**: Shows if client is connected

### Professional Best Practices

**Starting the Session**
1. **Greet the client warmly** when they join
2. **Check audio/video quality** - "Can you see and hear me clearly?"
3. **Address any technical issues** before beginning therapy
4. **Establish the same boundaries** as in-person sessions
5. **Begin with your usual opening routine**

**During the Session**
- **Maintain eye contact** by looking at the camera, not the screen
- **Use normal therapy techniques** - the platform shouldn't change your approach
- **Be patient with technology** - clients may need help
- **Use screen sharing** to show resources or worksheets
- **Take notes** as you normally would (outside the video interface)

**Ending the Session**
1. **Provide session summary** as usual
2. **Schedule next appointment** if needed
3. **Address any technical concerns** for future sessions
4. **Click "End Call"** to conclude
5. **Document session notes** in your usual system

## üõ†Ô∏è Advanced Features

### Screen Sharing
**When to Use:**
- Showing therapy worksheets or resources
- Reviewing homework assignments
- Demonstrating relaxation techniques
- Sharing educational materials

**How to Use:**
1. Click the "Screen Share" button
2. Choose what to share (entire screen, specific window, or browser tab)
3. Click "Share" when prompted
4. Client will see your shared content
5. Click "Stop Sharing" when finished

**Best Practices:**
- **Prepare materials in advance** - have documents ready
- **Share specific windows** rather than entire screen for privacy
- **Ask client if they can see clearly** before proceeding
- **Use large fonts** - text may appear smaller on client's screen

### Audio/Video Controls
**Camera Control:**
- Turn off video for audio-only sessions when appropriate
- Adjust camera angle for professional appearance
- Use virtual backgrounds if your environment isn't ideal

**Microphone Control:**
- Mute when not speaking if there's background noise
- Use headphones to prevent echo
- Adjust microphone sensitivity in browser settings

**Quality Settings:**
- Platform automatically adjusts for connection quality
- You can manually reduce video quality if connection is poor
- Audio-only mode available for very slow connections

## üë• Managing Client Experience

### Helping Clients with Technology

**Common Client Issues:**
1. **Can't see "Join Call" button** - Check payment status and timing
2. **Camera permission denied** - Guide them to browser settings
3. **Audio problems** - Help them check system volume and microphone
4. **Connection issues** - Suggest moving closer to WiFi router

**Your Role as Tech Support:**
- **Stay calm and patient** - technology anxiety is real
- **Provide simple, clear instructions** - avoid technical jargon
- **Have backup plans ready** - phone call, rescheduling
- **Reassure clients** - technical issues don't reflect on them

**Guiding Clients Through Setup:**
```
"I can see you're having trouble with your camera. Let's fix this together:
1. Look at the top of your browser - do you see a camera icon?
2. Click on that icon
3. Select 'Allow' for both camera and microphone
4. Now refresh the page by pressing F5
5. Try clicking 'Join Call' again"
```

### Building Rapport in Virtual Space

**Overcoming Virtual Barriers:**
- **Use the client's name frequently** to maintain connection
- **Acknowledge the technology** - "I know this feels different"
- **Be more expressive** - gestures and facial expressions may be less clear
- **Check in regularly** - "How is this working for you?"
- **Normalize technical difficulties** - "These things happen, let's work through it"

**Creating Therapeutic Presence:**
- **Maintain professional appearance** and background
- **Use appropriate lighting** so client can see your expressions
- **Minimize distractions** - close other applications, silence phone
- **Stay focused on the client** - avoid looking at yourself on screen
- **Use the same therapeutic techniques** you would in person

## üö® Troubleshooting and Support

### Common Technical Issues

**Client Can't Join:**
1. **Check payment status** - must be confirmed
2. **Verify session timing** - within allowed window
3. **Guide browser permission setup** - camera/microphone access
4. **Suggest browser refresh** or different browser (Chrome)

**Poor Connection Quality:**
1. **Ask client to move closer to WiFi router**
2. **Suggest closing other internet-using applications**
3. **Switch to audio-only mode** if video is too choppy
4. **Consider rescheduling** if connection is unusable

**Audio/Video Problems:**
1. **Check if other applications are using camera/microphone**
2. **Guide client through browser settings**
3. **Test with different devices** if available
4. **Use phone backup** if technical issues persist

### When to Escalate

**Contact Technical Support When:**
- Multiple clients report the same issue
- Platform-wide connectivity problems
- Billing/payment integration issues
- Security concerns or suspicious activity

**Emergency During Session:**
- **Continue session by phone** if possible
- **Reschedule if technical issues prevent therapy**
- **Document technical problems** for support team
- **Reassure client** that technical issues are not their fault
